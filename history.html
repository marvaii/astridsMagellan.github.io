Breif History of the Imjin War

This was a war fought by Korea and China against Japan

General Toyotomi Hideyoshi is credited with starting the Imjin War. He declared war on Korea after he had spent two decades directing campaigns to unite Japan (in Hawaii). After he unified his own land, he set his sights on China. To gain control of China, General Hideyoshi went after Korea because it was on the mainland and neighbored China. General Hideyoshi first sought to amend relations with Korea after a strained relationship caused by uncontrolled pirate attacks. He sought permission from Korea for passage into China (Museum). At this point in history, Korea was a vassal state of China and therefore refused to aid the Japanese. (Hawaii). However, King Seonjo of Korea sent envoys to gather information on Japanese plans going forward. Due to unclear reports, the King chose not to send reinforcements to the Korean Peninsula, leaving the people on the coast vulnerable.

Calculated Attacks of the Imjin War

The Japanese attacked Korea on two different occasions

The Japanese attacked Busan on April 13th, 1592, with approximately 16,000 Japanese soldiers. The Japanese prevailed with little difficulty, starting a seven-year conflict between them, Korea, and China. In just 15 days of war, the Joseon capital was conquered, but King Seonjo was able to flee to Uiju in northwestern Korea. The invasion by the Japanese left Korean farmers no choice but to create their own militias that went up against the Japanese (Hawaii). With the help of the hundreds of militias, the Korean military was able to counter the Japanese by intercepting a Japanese naval ship with supplies. The Korean naval forces were very skilled and compared to the Japanese. The Japanese occupation had many more soldiers, but due to having to deal with pirates for decades, the Koreans had an advantage on the water. The Korean Admiral Yi Sun-sin is said to be the first to build an ironclad ship, but there is no proof because this ship was lost many decades ago. As the War progressed, the Ming Dynasty sent reinforcements at the request of King Sonjo because the Japanese had reached the border between Korea and China. Together, the Chinese and Koreans were able to run off the Japanese to the South of Korea and negotiate. (museum).

The Imjin War is Over

The events that led to the war ending

When it was time to reach an agreement, the real demands of Hideyoshi never reached Emperor Ming. Instead, the Emperor received a falsified request pleading for forgiveness and requesting to become a vassal state of China. (Hawaii). China agreed to these terms, infuriating Hideyoshi. Thus, Hideyoshi launched his second attack on Korea again in 1597 with an army of 140,000 soldiers. It is believed that General Hideyoshi was able to win this battle because Admiral Yi was wrongly incarcerated. Even though they were victorious during the naval battle, they did not succeed in taking more of Korea because the Chinese army now backed the Korean military. Despite his initial success, not long after Toyotomi Hideyoshi died due to illness on September 18, 1598. His successor, Tokugawa Jeyasu, ordered the Japanese troops to retreat from Korea three months after Hideyoshi. After Admiral Yi was reinstated, he was shot and killed at the last battle between Korea and Japan. Admiral Yi was was hero to his people and one of the reasons that the Japanese could never gain control of Korean waters.

Breif History on World War II

The Influence of Japan

Shortly before WWII, the Japanese started to colonize Korea again. Once again, the goal is to take over China, but due to the Imjin War, they had the knowledge on how to take control of Korea. Once World War II starts, Japan is Allied with Russia and Germany. Later in the war, Japan attacked Pearl Harbor, prompting the United States to declare war on Japan. After the Japanese surrender and the war was over, Korea was divided at the 38th parallel, with the USSR controlling North Korea and the United Nations, along with the United States, controlling South Korea. (KWAR).

The Korean War

A Military Imbalance

The division was done to facilitate the surrender of Japan and also in order to reunify the Koreas eventually. North Korea was Communist, and South Korea was a democracy. Reunification was the goal when North Korea attacked South Korea on June 25, 1950, in the name of communism.

The North Korean leader Kim Il-sung, attacked with weapons from the Soviet Union and support from Communist China and wreaked havoc on South Korea (Cause). South Korea was not prepared at all for this war and suffered a lot of damage early on, and continued to do so until the tables turned when South Korea was able to take back Seoul.

A Cease Fire

The War is not Over

A ceasefire was able to be negotiated and brought back the DMZ at the 38th parallel. Nowadays, this area is considered one of the most heavily guarded places in the world. The South Korean people still speak very fondly of the idea of reunification.

After the war ended, the United States helped South Korea with reconstruction with financial aid, military aid, and goods. To further help South Korea, teachers and professors were educated in the United States. These aids allowed South Korea to better itself and create the advancement that has changed the lives of millions today. At present, the United States Military makes up a large part of the South Korean Military.
